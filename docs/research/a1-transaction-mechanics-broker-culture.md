# A1 — Thai Real-Estate Transaction Mechanics & Broker Culture

## 1. Scope

**Scope note.** This artifact gates our group/exclusivity mechanics, the quick-sale matching flow, the opt-in-to-publish design, and the trust/anti-spam gate. It documents how Thai residential/land sales actually close (offer → reservation/deposit → Land Office transfer), who customarily pays which fees, how commission and co-agent splits work in a no-MLS market, and — most importantly for us — exactly how brokers and owners use LINE groups for dealflow today, because our product is a digitization of that existing behavior. Findings are Thailand-wide unless flagged as Bangkok-specific or Northern/Chiang-Mai-specific. Where research contradicts a settled product decision, it is flagged with **[CONTRADICTS SETTLED ASSUMPTION]**.

## 2. Thought leaders & sources consulted

1. **DDProperty / PropertyGuru editorial (ddproperty.com)** — Thailand's largest property portal; its Thai-language "knowledge" articles (ค่านายหน้า, ค่าธรรมเนียมโอน, NPA) are the de-facto consumer reference, updated annually (2569/2026 editions cited). High signal on what ordinary Thai buyers/sellers actually believe and search for.
2. **Agentable.co + Shinyu Residence broker community (Thai)** — practitioner forums where working Thai agents debate "คอม 3% vs รับโค" (commission vs co-broking) and post first-person Co-Agent dispute post-mortems. Highest-signal source for actual etiquette, splits, and poaching norms — this is the behavior we digitize.
3. **Developer/bank consumer guides (Sansiri, AP Thai, SC Asset, Krungsri "the coach", GHBank)** — authoritative on transfer-fee mechanics and the 2025–2026 government fee-reduction window because they process thousands of transfers; Thai-language, current.
4. **Tilleke & Gibbins, Benoit & Partners, Siam Legal (legal firms)** — authoritative on the *legal* skeleton (reservation contract form, SPA, Land Office transfer, deposit law, 49% condo rule, leasehold). Used for the legal backbone, cross-checked against developer guides for real-world practice.
5. **Pantip threads + expat guides (ExpatDen, ASEANNOW, Ask Thailand)** — ground-truth on lived experience: "is co-broker even a thing here", agent distrust ("ทำไมนายหน้าถูกมองว่าไม่สุจริต"), rental deposit reality, and Chiang-Mai-specific foreigner leasehold practice.

## 3. Findings

1. **The close is a 3-stage flow: reservation (booking) → SPA + deposit → Land Office transfer day.** A *reservation agreement* (ใบจอง) with a booking fee of ~THB 10,000–50,000 or ~1% locks the property; a Sale & Purchase Agreement (SPA) is then signed (commonly within 7–30 days) with a deposit of typically ~5%; the balance is paid on transfer day. (Sunway Estates / Benoit & Partners, 2025; oceanwwp.com, 2025)
2. **For resale (the case relevant to us) the dominant instrument is the "สัญญาจะซื้อจะขาย" (contract-to-sell / earnest-money deposit).** Buyer puts down เงินมัดจำ (earnest money) and the parties agree to transfer at the Land Office within a window of **~1–3 months**, mainly to give the buyer time to get mortgage approval. Two matching copies, one witness per side. (SCB, 2024; lovethaihome.com contract template; tb.co.th, 2024)
3. **Transfer is registered in person at the Land Office (กรมที่ดิน); both parties must attend, and balance + fees are settled there that day.** The fee base is the **higher** of declared price or official appraised value (to block undervaluation). (Siam Legal; Magna Carta FAQ; 2024–2025)
4. **Standard government transaction costs:** 2% transfer fee; **either** 3.3% Specific Business Tax (SBT — if seller held <5 years / not registered in house book >1 year) **or** 0.5% stamp duty (if held >5 years); plus seller withholding tax (progressive, ~1%+). Total typically 2.5%–6%+ of appraised value. (Forbes & Partners 2025/2026; Sansiri; AP Thai 2569)
5. **There is no statutory split of who pays — it's negotiated and written into the SPA — but the customary default is the transfer fee split 50/50 (each pays 1%), seller bears SBT/withholding, buyer bears mortgage-registration (จดจำนอง).** Sellers sometimes absorb all fees as a promotion. (Krungsri "the coach", 2569; Sansiri; tooktee.com 2568)
6. **A government incentive cut transfer + mortgage fees to ~0.01% for residential property up to THB 7M, running to 30 June 2026 — but Thai nationals only.** This materially changes seller economics on sub-7M homes right now and is a live, expiring variable. (Forbes & Partners 2025/2026; krungsri.com 2569)
7. **Sales commission is ~3% nationwide, 3–5% in regional/resort markets (incl. Chiang Mai); rentals are "one month's rent" for a 12-month+ lease.** (DDProperty 2569; RE/MAX Thailand; Phuket Realtor; greenwayproperty.co.th)
8. **No MLS, no mandatory license. Most listings are "open" (สัญญาเปิด) — owners hand the same property to many agents at once and may sell it themselves.** Exclusive ("สัญญาปิด"/closed) mandates exist and are pushed by professional agencies (RE/MAX), but are the minority; under a closed mandate the owner owes commission even if they find the buyer themselves. (property2share; homed4u; RE/MAX; ddproperty)
9. **"Co-Agent" / "รับโค" (rap co) is the core cooperation primitive: one agent holds the property, another brings the buyer; they split commission, customarily 50/50, sometimes 60/40, agreed verbally up front.** (shinyu-residence; agentable.co; matching-property.com 2024)
10. **Co-Agent practice is fragile and trust-based; the canonical failure is being cut out or under-served.** Practitioner guidance is explicit: agree the % and the division of duties **before** starting; document it; and prefer the owner paying commission **in cash at signing** so it can be split on the spot rather than trusting one party to pay the other afterward. (shinyu-residence; agentable.co AgentClub post #1943 "Co-Agent หากไม่คุยให้ชัดเจนแต่แรก มักตามมาด้วยปัญหา" + the community's shared "สัญญา Co-Agent" written-split template, post #1379 — both live-verified 2026-06-12)
11. **Open listings with many agents cause visible price-cutting ("ตัดราคากัน") and listing duplication** — the same unit appears under many agents at different prices/photos, which is exactly the de-duplication and trust problem buyers complain about. (property2share; inno-home)
12. **LINE is the default channel for agent↔client and agent↔agent communication; agents respond faster on LINE than email, and co-broker networks run on LINE/portal feeds.** PropertyScout markets a network of 3,000+ co-agents fed listing-status updates — i.e., the "broker LINE group / shared feed" pattern already exists informally and is being productized. (ExpatDen 2024; PropertyScout)
13. **The trade has a real reputation/trust deficit in Thai eyes.** A Pantip thread literally asks "why is the broker profession seen as dishonest in Thai society"; scam guides note unlicensed agents operating in Facebook groups, fake/duplicate listings with stolen photos and below-market bait prices, and double-renting. One scam-advisory blog estimates foreign buyers lost ~THB 12B in 2024 — treat as an **uncorroborated single-source SEO estimate, not a fact**: it traces only to the Aster of Asia marketing page (which also makes the extraordinary, uncorroborated claim that "every third foreign transaction has a defect serious enough to cost the entire investment"), and a 2026-06-12 cross-check (SCMP, Bangkok Post, Nation Thailand) found no independent aggregate foreign-property-loss figure — the nearest hard numbers are single cases (e.g. SCMP cites one 20M-baht loss; Nation's 60B-baht is *all* online fraud, not foreign property). The directional point — bait/duplicate/stolen-photo fraud is real and named — stands on the verified Pantip + PropertyScout sources and does not depend on the 12B number. (Pantip 41642956; PropertyScout; Aster of Asia 2024–2025, uncorroborated)
14. **Owner-direct selling ("เจ้าของขายเอง") is a large, normalized channel** to avoid the 3% — via roadside signs, Facebook buy/sell groups, Kaidee, and owner-only portals (Meezub, ZmyHome, ThaiHometown). Chiang Mai owner-direct inventory is in the hundreds per portal. (Kaidee Chiang Mai; Meezub; ZmyHome; Trovit)
15. **Rental norms: 2 months deposit + 1 month advance (3 months cash up front) is typical via agents; deposit legally capped (≤2 months; ≤1 month if landlord owns >4 units under the 2018 consumer-protection notification); deposit returned within ~7 days of inspection; agent fee = 1 month's rent for 12-month+ leases.** (Sun Legal; KAHOUZE; sunrise-successful; ASEANNOW)
16. **Northern Thailand / Chiang Mai specifics:** foreigners cannot own land, so houses sell predominantly as **30-year leasehold (often "lease the land + buy the structure")**, with condo sales bound by the 49% foreign-quota rule; market is foreigner-and-investor-driven (China, Western retirees, digital nomads), prices ~30–40% below Bangkok, transfer/commission mechanics otherwise the same as the rest of Thailand. (chiangmai-properties.com 2025; Benoit & Partners; rumavi 2026)
17. **Distressed / quick-sale supply has an established mental model: NPA (ทรัพย์สินรอการขาย — bank-foreclosed, sold below market to clear fast) and owner "ขายด่วน/ขายต่ำกว่าราคาตลาด" listings.** Banks publish NPA inventory openly; investor demand for below-market assets is a known, named category — exactly the matching our quick-sale flow targets. (DDProperty NPA 2024; AP Thai; Krungsri Property)

## 4. Implications for us

- **F1/F2 → Listing schema must model the resale flow, not the new-build flow.** Capture `dealType` (sale | rent), and for sale a `saleStage` enum aligned to Thai practice: `available → reserved (มัดจำ) → under_contract (จะซื้อจะขาย) → transferred`. Reservation/earnest-money is the first commitment signal a group cares about.
- **F2/F3 → Add a `transferWindowDays` / expected-transfer hint (default 30–90).** The 1–3-month mortgage-contingent gap is when a listing is "soft-sold but not closed" — our exclusivity-window (~7 days) and "release publicly" mechanics should treat *reserved* differently from *transferred*.
- **F4/F5/F6 → Do NOT auto-compute or display a single "net to seller" / fee number as fact.** Fees are negotiated and there's a live Thai-national-only 0.01% window expiring 30 Jun 2026. If we show fees, show them as an editable estimate with the split assumption stated, and flag the incentive's nationality condition. Schema: optional `feeSplitNote` free-text, never a hard computed total presented as authoritative.
- **F7/F9/F10 → The opt-in-to-publish + co-agent reality maps to a first-class "Co-Agent / รับโค" affordance.** When a poster publishes/releases a listing, offer a structured "เปิดให้ co-agent" toggle with a `coAgentSplit` field (default 50/50, allow 60/40) and `coAgentTerms` note. This is the digitization of rap co; make the split explicit because verbal splits are the #1 dispute.
- **F8/F11 → Our group-private-by-default + exclusivity window IS the digital "open vs closed mandate."** Model a `listingMandate` (group_exclusive | open) and an `exclusivityExpiresAt`. The 7-day window = a soft closed-mandate inside the originating group; lapse → open. This is coherent with how owners already think (เปิด vs ปิด). **[PARTIALLY CONFIRMS — the open/closed (เปิด/ปิด) mandate distinction is a real, documented Thai convention; the *7-day time-boxed* version of it is unverified and may be our invention — see Confidence.]** The mandate concept is digitized from real practice; the specific timer is not.
- **F10 → Trust design: build the "agree the split before you start, in writing" norm into the UI.** A co-agent handshake should be a recorded, both-parties-acknowledged object (timestamp + agreed %), so the app is the neutral record the cash-at-signing custom currently substitutes for. This is a genuine wedge: we remove the "he didn't pay me afterward" failure.
- **F11/F13 → The LLM dedup/spam/quality gate is the headline trust feature, not a nice-to-have.** Open-listing duplication + stolen-photo fakes + bait pricing are the market's daily pain. Dedup must key on Land Office identifiers (chanote/deed no.), normalized address, geo, and image hashing — not just text. Surface a "verified once, deduped" badge.
- **F12 → LINE-first everywhere; the bot living in the group is the product, not a bolt-on.** Primary CTA is LINE chat; agent contact = LINE, never an email form as primary. Our passive-listen-in-group model is exactly where dealflow already happens.
- **F14 → Owner-direct must be a peer entry path, not an afterthought.** A `source`/`postedByRole` (broker | owner | investor) field; owner-direct posters get the bot-DM flow and the same dedup gate. Don't assume a broker is always in the loop. **[GO-TO-MARKET RISK — surface to founder]** F14 + Anti-pattern 4 show owners *actively* sell direct to dodge the 3% commission and resist exclusivity; this anti-commission, broker-skeptical demand is a structural headwind for any roadmap that monetizes *broker* dealflow subscriptions. It does not contradict a settled decision (owner-direct is already a first-class path here), but it means broker-subscription revenue cannot assume brokers sit between owner and buyer — the owner-direct path may cannibalize exactly the dealflow brokers would pay for.
- **F15 → Rental listings need their own fields:** `monthlyRent`, `depositMonths` (default 2), `advanceMonths` (default 1), `minLeaseMonths` (default 12), and an agent-fee = 1 month convention. Rental cards should show the "3 months up front" reality so it's not a surprise.
- **F16 → Chiang Mai/Northern build must support tenure type as a first-class field:** `tenure` (freehold | leasehold), `leaseYears` (default 30), and foreign-eligibility flags (condo 49% quota; land = no foreign freehold). English+Thai bilingual is non-optional given the foreigner-heavy buyer base.
- **F17 → The quick-sale flow has a native vocabulary: ขายด่วน / ต่ำกว่าราคาตลาด / NPA.** Use those labels in Thai copy. Quick-sale submissions should carry a `discountVsMarket` signal and route to vetted broker/investor pushes — matching an already-understood investor appetite.

## 5. Heuristics

- **DEAL-01:** Sale listings MUST carry a `saleStage` enum {available, reserved, under_contract, transferred} mirroring the Thai close (มัดจำ → จะซื้อจะขาย → โอน); a reviewer can confirm by checking the listing schema enum.
- **DEAL-02:** A listing entering `reserved`/`under_contract` MUST NOT be auto-released to other groups/public until either the exclusivity window lapses OR it reaches `transferred` — because the ~1–3-month mortgage gap is "soft-sold, not closed." Checkable in the release-state machine.
- **DEAL-03:** Any displayed transfer-fee/cost figure MUST render as an editable estimate with the split assumption labeled, and MUST flag the 0.01% incentive as Thai-national-only and expiring 30 Jun 2026 — never a single authoritative "total". Checkable on the fee UI/copy.
- **DEAL-04:** The default fee-split shown is transfer fee 50/50, seller pays SBT/withholding, buyer pays mortgage registration — and this default text MUST be overridable, not hard-coded as law. Checkable in copy + a `feeSplitNote` field existing.
- **DEAL-05:** Publishing/releasing a listing MUST offer a structured Co-Agent ("รับโค") option with an explicit `coAgentSplit` (default 50/50, presets incl. 60/40) — not free text only. Checkable on the publish flow.
- **DEAL-06:** A Co-Agent agreement MUST be recorded as a both-parties-acknowledged object with timestamp + agreed %, recorded at or before the co-agent intro — digitizing the documented "agree the split before you start, in writing" norm (Shinyu/Agentable post #1379 written-split template). *Design recommendation (not a documented norm): gate buyer-contact exchange until the split is acknowledged* — research supports "agree first, in writing," but blocking contact-exchange specifically is our design inference. Checkable in the co-agent data model.
- **DEAL-07:** Each listing MUST store `listingMandate` {group_exclusive, open} + `exclusivityExpiresAt`; group-exclusive is the default on passive capture. Checkable in schema + default value.
- **DEAL-08:** Primary contact CTA on every listing is LINE chat; phone is secondary; an email form is NEVER the primary contact. Checkable on the detail card.
- **DEAL-09:** Public-listing dedup MUST key on at least one Land Office identifier (chanote/deed number) OR normalized geo+address OR image hash — text-title matching alone is insufficient. Checkable in the dedup gate logic.
- **DEAL-10:** A `postedByRole` {broker, investor, owner} field MUST exist and owner-direct ("เจ้าของขายเอง") MUST be a supported first-class entry path with the same dedup/quality gate. Checkable in schema + onboarding flows.
- **DEAL-11:** Rental listings MUST capture `depositMonths` (default 2), `advanceMonths` (default 1), `minLeaseMonths` (default 12) and surface total cash-up-front; sale-only fields MUST NOT be required for rentals. Checkable on the rental form.
- **DEAL-12:** Listings MUST carry `tenure` {freehold, leasehold} with `leaseYears` (default 30 for leasehold) and foreign-eligibility flags; Chiang-Mai house listings default to leasehold-capable. Checkable in schema.
- **DEAL-13:** Quick-sale submissions MUST capture a below-market signal (`discountVsMarket` or asking-vs-appraised) and use native labels (ขายด่วน / NPA / ต่ำกว่าราคาตลาด) in Thai copy. Checkable on the quick-sale form + copy.
- **DEAL-14:** Sale commission default presented is 3% (allow 3–5% regional incl. Chiang Mai); rental agent fee default is 1 month's rent — both editable, never hidden/assumed. Checkable in commission fields.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- **Treating "reserved" as "sold/closed" and auto-publishing/releasing it** — ignores the 1–3-month mortgage-contingent gap; deals fall through and the listing is wrongly removed or wrongly freed to competitors.
- **Showing one authoritative fee/net-proceeds number** — fees are negotiated, vary by holding period, and the headline 0.01% rate is Thai-national-only and expires 30 Jun 2026; a hard number will be wrong and erode trust.
- **Letting the same property appear N times at N prices** (open-listing duplication / price-cutting "ตัดราคากัน") — the core buyer complaint; without chanote/geo/image dedup we reproduce the exact pain we claim to fix.
- **Forcing exclusive mandates as the default** — most Thai owners use open listings and many sell direct to dodge the 3%; a hard exclusivity requirement will repel supply.
- **Verbal/implicit co-agent splits** — the #1 documented Co-Agent dispute; if our app facilitates the intro but not the recorded split, we inherit the dispute instead of solving it.
- **Email-form-first or web-form-only contact** — Thai dealflow runs on LINE; a non-LINE primary CTA gets ignored.
- **Bait listings / stolen-photo fakes / below-market lures** passing the public gate — these already cost foreign buyers billions and define the market's distrust; our spam gate must catch them or our "trust" pitch is hollow.
- **One-size sale schema applied to rentals** — rentals need deposit/advance/lease-term, not transfer-fee/SBT fields; mixing them confuses both posters and buyers.

## 7. Confidence

**VERIFIED (cited, 2024–2026):**
- 3-stage close, reservation/earnest-money, 1–3-month transfer window (multiple legal + developer sources).
- Transfer fee 2%, SBT 3.3% vs stamp 0.5% rule, customary 50/50 fee split, 0.01% Thai-national incentive to 30 Jun 2026.
- Commission ~3% (3–5% regional), rental fee = 1 month, deposit 2 + advance 1, deposit-return ~7 days.
- No MLS / no mandatory license; open (เปิด) vs closed (ปิด) mandates; Co-Agent/รับโค 50/50–60/40; "agree split up front / cash at signing" etiquette.
- LINE as default channel; owner-direct channels (Kaidee/Facebook/Meezub/ZmyHome) large in Chiang Mai; Chiang Mai leasehold/49%-quota/foreigner-driven dynamics; NPA quick-sale category.

**PLAUSIBLE-BUT-UNVERIFIED (label honestly):**
- The *precise* in-group etiquette of broker LINE groups (who may re-post whose listing, how poaching is socially policed, whether "first to post owns it" conventions exist) — inferred from co-agent forum norms + general LINE usage, NOT directly observed in real broker groups. **This is our weakest area and the one our product most depends on.**
- Whether an informal "exclusivity window" already exists socially in groups (our 7-day mechanic) — plausible by analogy to closed mandates, but I found no source documenting a time-based in-group convention; it may be our invention rather than a digitization. **[POSSIBLE UNVERIFIED ASSUMPTION — flag to founder.]**
- Real prevalence of co-agent cooperation in *Chiang Mai broker groups specifically* vs Bangkok/Phuket (sources skew Bangkok/resort).
- Exact current investor appetite/pricing for owner-submitted (non-bank) distressed quick-sale, as opposed to bank NPA.

## 8. Ask the market

1. In your actual broker/owner LINE groups, **what is the unwritten rule when someone posts a listing** — can another member re-post it elsewhere, must they ask, is there a "the poster owns it for N days" norm? (Validates DEAL-07 exclusivity window — possibly our invention.)
2. **When a deal is co-agented in the group today, how is the split actually agreed and enforced** — verbal? screenshot? cash at signing? Has anyone been burned? (Validates DEAL-05/06.)
3. **How is poaching handled socially** — what happens to someone who contacts another member's client/owner directly? Are they removed from the group? (Defines our trust/penalty design.)
4. For sale listings that go "มัดจำ/reserved", **how long until they actually transfer, and how often do they fall through** at the mortgage stage? (Calibrates DEAL-02 release timing.)
5. Do your owners/brokers expect the app to show **fee/commission math**, or is that something they keep deliberately vague/negotiable? (Validates DEAL-03/04 — maybe we should show *less*.)
6. In Chiang Mai specifically, **what share of your dealflow is leasehold-to-foreigner vs Thai freehold**, and does that change the LINE-group etiquette? (Validates DEAL-12 + Northern assumptions.)
7. For quick-sale, **do investors in your network actually want owner-submitted distressed deals**, or is that demand satisfied by bank NPA already? (De-risks the quick-sale flow.)
