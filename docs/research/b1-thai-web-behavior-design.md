# B1 — Thai Web User Behavior & Design Patterns (2026)

**Scope note.** This artifact gates our entire design system and auth flows: which login method is primary (LINE Login vs phone-OTP vs email), how contact CTAs are ordered on a listing, which trust signals the listing card and detail page must surface, the typography stack (Thai font choice, line-height, word-breaking strategy), color/cultural defaults, information-density posture, and how Thai listing copy/SEO should be phrased. Decisions here propagate into schema fields (contact channels, verification badges), the Astro/Tailwind/shadcn token system, and bilingual copy rules. It does NOT cover legal/PDPA, payments, or AVM — those are separate artifacts.

## Thought leaders & sources consulted

- **DataReportal (Digital 2025: Thailand, Kepios / Simon Kemp)** — the canonical annual digital-behavior dataset for Thailand; de-duplicated cross-platform methodology, published Mar 2025 with Jan 2025 baseline. Highest-signal for adoption/penetration numbers; cited by virtually every credible Thai marketing report.
- **Cadson Demak / Anuthin Wongsunkakon** — Bangkok type foundry (founded 2002) that authored most of the modern Thai web typefaces in use today (incl. the Google Fonts Thai families like Prompt, Sarabun-adjacent work, and brand commissions for Singha, Bangkok Bank, ThaiBev). Anuthin founded Southeast Asia's first typography conference (BITS, 2010) and teaches at Chulalongkorn/Bangkok University. The deepest available authority on looped-vs-loopless and Thai screen legibility.

Primary research; the perceptual-study version was presented at ATypI (Copenhagen 2025) co-credited with **Mint Tantisuwanna**, with research assistants from Chulalongkorn University (Sureeporn Khampaeng, Duantemdoung Dethsuphar, Phuttharaksa Lanoi). This is the strongest single source for our font decision. (Earlier drafts said "180 readers" — the source's own total is 218 across the three experiments; corrected.)
- **Designil (designil.com)** — long-running Thai-language UX/design education site (articles + courses + paid membership). Strong signal because it documents *real-world testing* of Thai web fonts and system-font fallback behavior across OSes — practitioner depth, not listicle. ## Findings

1. **LINE is effectively universal in Thailand and mobile-bound.** 56M monthly active LINE users (Jan 2025) = 78.2% of total population and 85.7% of internet users; 92% access LINE on mobile. (DataReportal, Digital 2025 Thailand, Mar 2025; Fortune Thailand 2025.)
2. **Thailand is a mobile-first, near-saturated internet market.** 65.4M internet users (91.2% penetration), 99.5M mobile connections (139% of population), ~98% of users browse via phone; median mobile download 61.2 Mbps. (DataReportal, Jan 2025.)
3. **Google owns search almost completely; SEO = Google SEO.** ~99.5% search share (Statcounter, 99.52% at May 2026 — the live slug shows the latest month, so the exact month/decimal drifts; treat as "~99.5%, stable"); >80% of searches are mobile. Bing/DuckDuckGo/Yandex are rounding error. (Statcounter; Ittiphat Agency, 2024–2025.)
4. **LINE Login is the low-friction identity Thai users already trust** — one-tap authorization vs filling a form; LIFF lets web apps run inside LINE's in-app browser. *Mechanism (not a Thai-specific measurement):* login/signup friction is a recognized abandonment driver in the general UX literature, and the global e-commerce cart-abandonment baseline sits around ~70% (Baymard/Corbado lineage) — but that figure is **generic-global, not Thai and not segmented by auth method**, and no source attributes a specific share of it to login. The claim we actually rely on is narrower and *inferred from LINE's near-universality* (F1): a one-tap LINE authorization removes a form/SIM-OTP step that Thai users would otherwise face, so it should out-convert email/phone signup here. We have no head-to-head Thai auth-method abandonment study (see Confidence). (LINE Developers docs; Corbado/Baymard — generic-global; LINE abandoned-cart practitioner writing for Thailand, MCIX 2026.)
5. **Thai users are actively scam-wary; verification marks matter.** Thailand logged >1M online-fraud cases; OCPB pushes "Dee-Delivery" COD protections; e-commerce sellers are urged to surface official **DBD Registered / DBD Verified** marks and buyers are coached to check phone + address + reviews before trusting a seller. *Scope caveat:* DBD Registered/Verified is a **Dept. of Business Development registration mark for registered e-commerce business operators** (Verified has Silver/Gold/Platinum tiers) — it is NOT a real-estate/agent trust mark, and most individual Chiang Mai brokers/owners in our private-group dealflow are not DBD-registered entities. So DBD is the **mental model** for "an official-looking verification mark Thais expect," not a system we integrate against. (Tech For Good Institute; Bangkok Post 2024; OCPB; DBD / Thai e-commerce-registration guides.)
6. **Empirical Thai loop study (fieldwork 2024, published 2025): looped wins for body, but the effect is mostly *perceived* legibility, and age splits the result.** Looped gave a small but significant continuous-reading speed advantage and was perceived as more readable regardless of format; younger readers found looped *more* challenging while older readers struggled with loopless (i.e. younger skew loopless, older skew looped) — consistent with younger exposure to loopless signage/digital. Loops read as "classical/formal," loopless as "modern/unusual." (Typotheque / Mangas, fieldwork early 2024, published 15 Feb 2025; perceptual-study version co-credited Tantisuwanna, presented ATypI Copenhagen 2025; study conducted at Chulalongkorn University with Chula research assistants.)
7. **Sarabun is the de-facto Thai body standard; LINE Seed TH is loopless/geometric "modern."** Sarabun (looped humanist, generous x-height, low contrast) is the government/long-form reading default; Noto Sans Thai ships both looped and loopless variants with reliable diacritic stacking; LINE Seed TH is loopless and reads as clean/modern but is weaker for dense long-form Thai. (Google Fonts; Designil; Thaigraph; Wikipedia Thai typography.)
8. **Thai has no inter-word spaces — line-breaking is a real engineering problem.** Thai uses spaces only between phrases; correct wrapping needs *dictionary-based segmentation* (ICU). Browsers that use ICU handle Thai wrapping; `word-break: keep-all` still allows dictionary breaks; otherwise text wraps mid-word or relies on authored zero-width spaces (U+200B). (W3C i18n; ICU docs; CSSWG issue #2455.)
9. **Thai property search is type + location + condition, phrased in Thai.** Real query shapes: `บ้านมือสอง` (second-hand house), `คอนโดใกล้ฉัน` (condo near me), `ขายบ้าน เชียงใหม่`, `บ้านเช่า เชียงใหม่`, `ที่ดิน เชียงใหม่`. Dominant portals (DDProperty, Kaidee, LivingInsider, ChiangMaiBaan) rank on these exact Thai compound phrases. (Live SERP inspection; DDProperty; Kaidee; Pantip property threads.)
10. **On Thai property portals, the primary contact rail is phone + LINE, not email.** Agent directories surface agency name, agent photo, phone, and LINE; DDProperty lists LINE/WhatsApp contact on agents and is the #1 portal (~3.5M monthly visits). Email exists but is never the primary CTA. (DDProperty agent directory; LivingInsider; Pantipmarket.)
11. **The "dense Thai website" trope is real but splitting.** Thai consumer-facing portals still skew information-dense (listing pages pack price, specs, agent, map, related), yet the broader 2025 direction in Thai consumer apps (LINE, banking, super-apps) is toward cleaner card-based layouts. Dense ≠ good by default; density is tolerated for *comparison/decision* screens, disliked for onboarding/landing. The card-UI shift is documented in Thai-language practitioner writing (Designil's Card UI Design piece; UXUI Thailand / SennaLabs 2025 trend syntheses both put card-based + functional-minimalism as the dominant Thai direction). Note this is a *directional* read of trend writing + portal observation, not a controlled Thai study (see Confidence). (Thai property portals observed; Designil — *Card Design — User Interface*, Thai; UXUI Thailand / SennaLabs 2025 Thai trend syntheses.)
12. **Color in Thai branding leans trust-blue/green; cultural color meaning is nuanced, not cliché.** Thai branding writing treats blue as trust/safety, green as freshness/calm/money; purple can read as royalty *or* widow's mourning depending on context; black is not auspicious. The day-color (เลขสีประจำวัน) folklore exists but is NOT a hard constraint for a commerce brand — treat it as optional flavor, not a rule. (GiantPrint; Sumeka; DIGITORY; Thai branding/color-psychology articles.)

## Implications for us

- **F1/F4 → Auth:** LINE Login is the PRIMARY auth button on web and the only one inside the LIFF mini-app. Email/Google are secondary fallbacks below it. No phone-OTP as a primary path (adds SIM friction LINE already removes). Schema: `identity.provider ∈ {line, google, email}` with `line` as default/expected.
- **F1/F10 → Contact rail + schema:** A listing's contact CTA order is **LINE chat → phone → (everything else)**; email is never the primary CTA. Schema needs `contact.line` (id or deep link), `contact.phone`, optional `contact.email`. The "chat with poster" button should deep-link into LINE (we already do MINI App deep links — reuse).
- **F5/F10 → Trust signals on the card/detail:** Surface a **verification badge** for vetted broker/investor — visually echoing the *mental model* of an official DBD-style trust mark, but it is **our own vetting attestation, not a DBD lookup/integration** (don't build a DBD API call; most of our brokers aren't DBD-registered entities). Plus real agent **photo + agency name** and a **real phone/LINE**. Schema: `poster.verified: bool`, `poster.role`, `poster.displayPhoto`, `listing.photoSource ∈ {real, render}` so renders can be labeled (renders without a label erode trust here).
- **F6/F7 → Typography tokens:** Body Thai = a **looped** humanist (Sarabun or Noto Sans Thai looped) for listing descriptions/long-form; headings/UI labels may use a cleaner/loopless face (Noto Sans Thai loopless or LINE Seed TH) for the "modern" read. Do NOT set loopless as the long-form body default. Provide a webfont with a system-font fallback stack (Designil's tested fallbacks).
- **F6 → line-height:** Thai stacks vowels/tone marks above and below the baseline, so set a **larger line-height than Latin** (target ~1.6–1.8 for Thai body) and add slight letter-spacing for Sarabun-family faces; never reuse a Latin-tuned 1.4.
- **F8 → Word-breaking (engineering):** Wrap Thai text with dictionary segmentation. For Astro SSR React islands: rely on the browser's ICU line-breaking (set `lang="th"` and `word-break: normal`), and for known long unbreakable strings inject zero-width spaces (U+200B) server-side. Do not depend on CSS `word-break: break-word` for Thai — it breaks mid-syllable and looks broken to Thai readers.
- **F3/F9 → SEO:** Localize URL slugs, titles, and H1s to the Thai compound phrases users actually type (`ขายบ้าน-เชียงใหม่`, `คอนโดให้เช่า-เชียงใหม่`, `บ้านมือสอง-เชียงใหม่`). Optimize for Google only and mobile-first; bilingual pages should have a Thai canonical for Thai queries.
- **F11 → Density posture:** Landing/onboarding = clean card layout (don't out-dense the portals). Listing-detail and search-results = allow higher density (price, specs, agent, map together) because that's a comparison/decision screen Thais expect to be information-rich.
- **F12 → Color:** Default brand toward trust-blue / fresh-green; avoid purple as a primary brand color and black-dominant funeral connotations; do not hard-code day-color superstition into the system.

## Heuristics

- **TH-01:** LINE Login is the primary auth CTA on every login/signup screen (web + mini-app); Google/email appear only as smaller secondary options below it. Phone-OTP is never the primary path. *(Check: top-most, largest auth button is LINE.)*
- **TH-02:** A listing's contact CTA order is always LINE chat first, phone second; email is never offered as the primary contact action. *(Check: first contact button = LINE; no email-only contact.)*
- **TH-03:** Every public listing card shows at least one human trust signal — real agent/owner photo OR agency name OR a verification badge — and never relies on text alone. *(Check: card renders a person/agency identity element.)*
- **TH-04:** Vetted broker/investor listings display a visible verification badge distinct from unvetted listings. The badge is **our own vetting attestation styled like an official trust mark** (DBD is only the mental model) — NOT a DBD registration lookup. *(Check: schema `poster.verified` drives a visible badge; no DBD API dependency.)*
- **TH-05:** Photos that are renders/CGI are labeled as such; un-labeled photos are treated as real and must be real. *(Check: `photoSource=render` renders a visible label.)*
- **TH-06:** Long-form Thai body text (listing descriptions) uses a LOOPED Thai face (Sarabun or Noto Sans Thai looped), not a loopless one. *(Check: computed font-family on description block is looped.)*
- **TH-07:** Thai body text uses line-height ≥ 1.6 (and never inherits a Latin-tuned line-height below 1.5). *(Check: computed line-height on Thai paragraphs.)*
- **TH-08:** Thai text containers set `lang="th"` and rely on ICU/dictionary line-breaking; `word-break: break-word`/`break-all` is never applied to Thai prose. *(Check: no break-all on Thai blocks; lang attr present.)*
- **TH-09:** The site is designed mobile-first and tested on a phone viewport before desktop; no critical CTA requires desktop width. *(Check: all primary flows usable at 360–390px wide.)*
- **TH-10:** SEO slugs/titles/H1 for catalog pages use the Thai compound phrases users type (type + location, e.g. `ขายบ้าน เชียงใหม่`), not English-only or transliterated terms. *(Check: Thai-language H1/title on Thai catalog pages.)*
- **TH-11:** Onboarding/landing screens use clean card layouts; high information density is reserved for search-results and listing-detail (comparison/decision) screens. *(Check: landing is not a dense table; detail may be.)*
- **TH-12:** Brand primary color is trust-blue or fresh-green family; purple is not the primary brand color and black is not the dominant surface. *(Check: primary token hue.)*
- **TH-13:** Headings/short UI labels may use a loopless/geometric Thai face (Noto Sans Thai loopless or LINE Seed TH) for a modern read, but this face is never the long-form body default. *(Check: heading font ≠ body font when loopless is used.)*
- **TH-14:** Bilingual UI keeps Thai as the default rendering for Thai users; English is a toggle, not the default, on the public catalog. *(Check: default locale resolves to th for Thai Accept-Language.)*

## Anti-patterns

- **Email-form-as-primary-contact.** A "send the agent an email" form as the main CTA — Thai property portals don't do this; it reads as foreign/low-trust and suppresses contact rates.
- **Phone-OTP gate before browsing.** Forcing SIM-based OTP signup before anyone can browse, when LINE Login removes that friction entirely. Drives abandonment.
- **Loopless Thai for dense long-form body.** Using LINE Seed TH / a loopless face for paragraph listing descriptions — perceived as less readable by all ages and harder for older buyers.
- **Latin line-height on Thai.** Reusing a 1.4 line-height tuned for English, which collides Thai upper/lower vowels and tone marks and looks cramped/broken.
- **`word-break: break-all` on Thai.** Mid-syllable wrapping that produces nonsense breaks to a Thai reader; a frequent bug in Tailwind setups that bolt break-all on globally.
- **Unlabeled renders.** Showing developer CGI/renders as if real photos — in a scam-wary market this destroys trust when the buyer arrives and reality differs.
- **English-only SEO/slugs.** Ranking pages on transliterated/English terms while Thai buyers search in Thai compound phrases — invisible to the actual query stream.
- **Out-densing the portals on the landing page.** Cramming everything onto the homepage because "Thai sites are dense" — density is tolerated on decision screens, not on first impression.

## Confidence

**VERIFIED (cited, current 2024–2026):**
- LINE/internet/mobile penetration figures (DataReportal Jan 2025).
- Google ~99.5% search share, mobile-first search (Statcounter, 99.52% at May 2026; live slug shows latest month so the exact figure drifts month to month — not a fixed snapshot).
- Looped-vs-loopless empirical findings incl. age split and perceived-legibility effect (Typotheque/Mangas, fieldwork 2024 / published 2025; 218 participants across 3 experiments, run at Chulalongkorn University).
- Thai has no inter-word spaces; ICU dictionary segmentation needed for correct wrapping (W3C/ICU).
- Sarabun = de-facto Thai body/government standard; LINE Seed TH is loopless (Google Fonts, Designil, Thaigraph).
- Scam-wariness + DBD verification-mark expectation (Tech For Good Institute, OCPB, Bangkok Post).
- Property portals present LINE/phone-first contact, agent photo/agency, verification (DDProperty/LivingInsider/Kaidee observed).

**PLAUSIBLE-BUT-UNVERIFIED (labeled honestly):**
- The specific ~1.6–1.8 Thai line-height range is a craft consensus from practitioner writing, not a single cited measurement — treat as a starting default to test, not proven.
- "LINE Login is what most Thai users actually pick over email/Google for a *real-estate* site" is inferred from LINE's universality + frictionless one-tap claims; I found no head-to-head abandonment study segmented by Thai auth method, so the *relative* abandonment ranking is plausible, not measured. The ~70% cart-abandonment figure cited in Finding 4 is **generic-global (Baymard/Corbado), not Thai and not login-segmented** — it supports the *mechanism* (friction drives abandonment), not the specific magnitude of the LINE-vs-email gap here.
- The density "splitting toward cleaner cards" claim blends Thai-portal observation with general 2025–2026 trend writing; the Thai-specific direction is directional, not a controlled study.
- Exact share of Thai buyers who refuse to contact via email (vs just preferring LINE) is not quantified in sources.

## Ask the market

- In your test LINE groups, when a buyer/broker reaches out about a listing, what channel do they actually use first — LINE 1:1, phone call, or anything else? (Confirms TH-02 ordering.)
- Do your vetted brokers WANT a visible "verified" badge, or do some prefer to look like independent owners? (Affects TH-04 prominence.)
- Would Chiang Mai owners/brokers accept LINE Login as the only mini-app identity, or do some expect a phone number flow? (Stress-tests TH-01.)
- For long Thai descriptions, do your users find Sarabun-style looped text clearly more readable than LINE Seed on their phones — quick A/B with 5–10 brokers? (Validates TH-06 against your actual audience age skew.)
- Are renders common in your dealflow (off-plan/new builds), and would labeling them as renders feel honest or feel like an accusation to your brokers? (Affects TH-05 copy.)
- What exact Thai phrases do your brokers use to describe Chiang Mai sub-areas (e.g. หางดง, สันทราย, นิมมาน) — these should become SEO slugs and search synonyms. (Feeds TH-10.)
- **TH-14 makes a call the product brief left open:** the brief says "launching Thai+English" without naming a default; TH-14 sets **Thai as the default** (English a toggle) for Thai-Accept-Language users. Is that intended, or do you want English-default for some segments (e.g. expat/foreign-buyer landing pages in Chiang Mai)? (Confirms TH-14 — a decision this artifact is making for you.)
